Offline Survival Manual

Tap on any emergency situation below to view step-by-step survival protocols. This manual works 100% offline.

🌍 Earthquakes & Collapses

1. Caught indoors during active shaking

Cause: Tectonic plates shifting causing violent structural vibration.

Immediate Action: DROP, COVER, and HOLD ON. Drop to your hands and knees. Cover your head and neck under a sturdy table or desk. Hold on to your shelter until shaking stops.

Guideline: Do NOT run outside during the shaking; most injuries occur from falling glass and exterior facades.

2. Trapped under heavy debris

Cause: Roof or wall collapse trapping the victim.

Immediate Action: Cover your mouth with clothing to prevent dust inhalation. Do NOT light a match (gas leak risk). Tap on a pipe or wall rhythmically so rescuers can hear you. Only shout as a last resort to save energy and avoid inhaling toxic dust.

3. Strong smell of gas after an earthquake

Cause: Ruptured underground gas lines.

Immediate Action: Open windows immediately. Evacuate the premises. Do not flip ANY electrical switches (even lights), as the tiny spark can ignite the gas.

4. Driving during an earthquake

Immediate Action: Pull over to the side of the road safely, away from overpasses, bridges, and power lines. Stay inside the vehicle with your seatbelt fastened until the shaking stops.

🌊 Severe Flooding

5. Water rapidly entering a single-story home

Cause: Flash flooding or breached dams.

Immediate Action: Move immediately to the roof or highest possible point. DO NOT climb into a closed attic; you risk becoming trapped by rising water. Take a brightly colored cloth to signal helicopters.

6. Vehicle stalled in moving floodwater

Immediate Action: If water is rising inside the car, abandon it immediately and seek higher ground. Just 6 inches of moving water can knock you down, and 2 feet can sweep away most vehicles. If doors won't open, roll down or break the window.

7. Forced to walk through floodwaters

Guideline: If absolutely necessary, walk where the water is NOT moving. Use a heavy stick to check the firmness of the ground in front of you and to avoid open manholes or debris.

8. Risk of waterborne electrocution

Cause: Submerged electrical outlets or fallen power lines.

Immediate Action: Turn off the main power breaker if you can do so while standing in a dry area. NEVER enter a flooded basement if the water is covering electrical outlets.

🌀 Cyclones & Hurricanes

9. Roof begins to tear off during cyclone

Cause: Extreme pressure differentials and high wind speeds.

Immediate Action: Immediately take shelter under a heavy mattress, a sturdy table, or inside a windowless interior room (like a bathroom or closet) on the lowest floor.

10. Windows blowing inwards from pressure

Immediate Action: Stay far away from all glass. Keep interior doors closed to prevent pressure from building up and blowing out the roof.

11. The storm suddenly stops completely

Cause: The "Eye of the Storm" is passing over.

Guideline: DO NOT go outside. The calm is deceptive. The most violent winds (the eyewall) will return suddenly from the opposite direction. Remain in your shelter.

12. Approaching coastal storm surge

Cause: Ocean water pushed inland by hurricane winds.

Immediate Action: This is the deadliest part of a cyclone. You cannot outrun it. Evacuate vertically (move to the 2nd or 3rd floor of a concrete building) immediately.

🔥 Fire & Smoke

13. Trapped in a smoke-filled room

Cause: Structural fire producing toxic carbon monoxide.

Immediate Action: Get down on your hands and knees and crawl. The cleanest air is 12 to 24 inches above the floor. Cover your nose/mouth with a wet cloth if possible.

14. Clothing catches on fire

Immediate Action: STOP, DROP, and ROLL. Cover your face with your hands, drop to the ground, and roll back and forth to smother the flames. Do not run, as oxygen feeds the fire.

15. Door handle feels hot to the touch

Guideline: DO NOT OPEN THE DOOR. Fire is directly on the other side. Stuff clothes or towels under the door crack to stop smoke. Open a window and signal for help using a flashlight or light-colored cloth.

16. Trapped by approaching wildfire

Immediate Action: If driving, roll up windows and close air vents. If on foot, seek a body of water, a cleared area with no vegetation, or crouch in a ditch and cover yourself with soil or a fire-resistant blanket.

🚑 Medical Trauma

17. Severe arterial bleeding

Cause: Deep lacerations from glass or debris.

Immediate Action: Apply direct, heavy pressure with a clean cloth. If bleeding doesn't stop, apply a tourniquet 2-3 inches above the wound (not on a joint) and note the exact time it was applied.

18. Suspected spinal injury / Broken neck

Cause: Falling debris or extreme impact.

Immediate Action: DO NOT MOVE THE VICTIM unless there is an immediate, deadly threat (like fire). Keep their head and neck perfectly still to prevent permanent paralysis.

19. Severe Dehydration / Heatstroke

Cause: Lack of clean water post-disaster.

Immediate Action: Move the person to shade. Cool them rapidly by applying cold wet cloths to their neck, armpits, and groin. Give them sips of water if conscious.

20. Person is unconscious and not breathing

Immediate Action: Begin Hands-Only CPR immediately. Push hard and fast in the center of the chest at a rate of 100-120 beats per minute (to the beat of the song "Stayin' Alive") until help arrives.
